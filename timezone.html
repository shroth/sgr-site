Planning & Process


I identified a problem with our global society - family and friends are living across multpile time zones, and people find it difficult to remember the time differences. For class, I decided to tackle this problem and design an app that would help keep family and friends connected: Connecting Time Zones.

To begin, I wrote an interview guide and conducted (and recorded) user research to learn more about the challenges people face today. I asked about the frustrations of current communication habits, as well as what features were appreciated. I researched the competitors further and came up with a feature inventory comparing my app, which was aiming to solve previously mentioned frustrations, to other forms of communication. I conducted a second round of user interviews to determine which features had the highest impact and were most expected by users.


The key features of my app were that it automatically calculated and displayed the current time for the person being contacted, and allowed for delayed sending so that the message could be received during waking hours. It turned out that users were afraid to send messages and have the notification noise wake the other person. This led them to either reduce the number of messages they sent or forget what they wanted to say when they were ready to write a message. At this point I was also able to craft user personas and draw out the site map and user flow of the app.

The next step was lo-fi usability testing. I sketched out the app on index cards and recorded users trying it out. Additionally, I conducted card sorting. After each round of user testing, I made small changes to the design to improve the user flow.

Finally, hi-fi designs were created in Sketch and Invision. Further user testing was conducted again until a final design was reached. Below is an example of transitioning from index cards into an annotated higher-fidelity wireframe.



Final Project


The final project included a working, connected example within Invision as well as a detailed presentation through the process from beginning to end. A final tabular design with labeled buttons instead of stand-alone symbols was selected since the final user population was expected to include a large portion of the older generation who may not be as facile with apps. For example, when a plus sign was tested to signify writing a new message, this created confusion for some users. Instead, the final design includes the written option 'New'.
