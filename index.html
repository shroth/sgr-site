About Me


My path to be a UX Designer and Front-End Developer has been unique and exciting. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Engineering majoring in Bioengineering and minoring in Engineering Entrepreneurship at the University of Pennsylvania. I led a team through the design process to create a patent-pending, award-winning medical device. I developed the skills to work effectively as part of a team and independently, learn new techniques quickly, work under pressure, and iterate until the problem is solved.


Post-grad, I worked at a healthcare consulting company for two years where I learned the intricacies of the healthcare system. It was important to keep in mind the overarching goal for the client, as well as develop empathy for each stakeholderâ€™s key concerns. By conducting in-depth interviews and mapping patients flows I was able to determine pain points for each party and determine an optimal solution. My ability to organize my takeaways graphically and textually has made me an effective communicator.

My true passion rests in crafting intuitive, aesthetically pleasing, and effective user experiences. I delved into UX and took classes in Visual Design and Front-End Development. I used these skills to design and code this site myself. To deepen my skillset within UX, I attended the rigorous User Experience Design Immersive at General Assembly. There, I was able to practice the central competencies as well as expand my expertise.


These experiences have taught me to be a creative quick thinker, problem solver, and develop empathy for multiple groups.
