About Me


My path to be a UX Designer and Front-End Developer has been unique and exciting. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Engineering studying Bioengineering and minoring in Engineering Entrepreneurship at the University of Pennsylvania. I developed the skills to work effectively as a team, learn on the job, work under pressure, and iterate until the problem is solved.


Post-grad, I worked at a healthcare consulting company for two years. It was important to keep in mind the overarching goal for the client, as well as develop empathy for each stakeholderâ€™s key concerns. By conducting in-depth interviews with users I was able to determine pain points for each party and determine an optimal solution. I also continued to hone my ability to breakdown a problem into its component parts and work with a team to develop the best solutions. I became proficient in thinking of novel ways to visually display our findings in PowerPoint, as well.

My true passion rests in crafting intuitive, aesthetically pleasing, and effective user experiences. I delved into UX and took classes in Visual Design and Front-End Development. I used these skills to design and code this site myself. To deepen my skillset within UX, I attended the User Experience Design Immersive at General Assembly. There, I was able to practice the central competencies as well as expand my expertise.


These experiences have taught me to be a creative quick thinker, problem solver, and develop empathy for multiple groups.
