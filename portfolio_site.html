Planning & Process


|| Wireframing & Testing

The first step in designing this website was to prioritize the content . Since the goal is to highlight my work, I wanted the portfolio section to be emphasized the most on the home page. Secondly, I described my past experiences, and thirdly provided a way to contact me. Contact information is commonly found at the bottom of sites, so it seemed practical to employ this user-familiar pattern. I mocked-up a low fidelity version of both the mobile (pictured) and desktop version.

I added color and some imagery to create a higher fidelity mock-up. It was important to me that my site have an open, welcoming feel, so I chose to have a navigation bar with a light, neutral color in a warm tone. I narrowed it down to two choices: a teak rose (first image) and a sand peach (second image). After user testing, I learned that the sand peach (second image) was less distracting and more inviting.

Besides choosing typography and a color scheme, I wanted to brand myself and design a logo to represent me. I played around with various arrangements, combining my initials or adjusting the character spacing within my name. My first design was a perfect example of bad idea. I landed on the final design of a big 'S', and my other two initials stacked on top of each other. I liked the compact simplicity of it.

|| Coding

Once the mock-ups were drawn and typography selected, I dived into coding. I began by templating the pages with image place holders and lorem ipsum. I leveraged Bootstrap for efficiency, but added many of my own customizations.

Before I inserted my actual content, I added some additional media queries so that my site would be responsive. For example, I coded for images to take up more space on mobile, adjusted font sizes, and adjusted the navigation bar. On my portfolio pages (such as this), I removed my logo from the top left, and instead maximized navigation space for the links. This makes it obvious and easy for mobile users to return to the main page or jump to a different section on the page.

The final challenge for my base site was accounting for the fixed header. When linking to sections on the same page or on a different page, the fixed header requires a data offset.
