Planning & Process


The first step in designing this website was to prioritize the content. Since the goal is to highlight my work, I wanted the portfolio section to be emphasized the most on the home page. Secondly, I described my past experiences, and thirdly provided a way to contact me. Contact information is commonly found at the bottom of sites, so it seemed practical to employ this user-familiar pattern. I mocked-up a low fidelity version of both the mobile (pictured) and desktop version.

I added color and some imagery to create a higher fidelity mock-up. It was important to me that my site have an open, welcoming feel, so I chose to have a navigation bar with a light, neutral color in a warm tone. I narrowed it down to two choices: a teak rose (first image) and a sand peach (second image). After user testing, I learned that the sand peach (second image) was less distracting and more inviting.


Once the mock-ups were drawn and typography selected, I dived into coding. I began by templating the pages with image place holders and lorem ipsum. I leveraged Bootstrap for efficiency, but added many of my own customizations. Before I inserted my actual content, I added some additional media queries so that my site would be responsive. For example, I coded for images to take up more space on mobile, adjusted font sizes, and adjusted the navigation bar. On my portfolio pages (such as this), I removed my logo from the top left, and instead inserted a home tab on the mobile viewport. This makes it obvious and easy for mobile users to return to the main page.



Final Project


You are viewing the final site! I was proud to accomplish my goals within my timeframe. The final site is responsive, functional, and effective at guiding a user through the site.
